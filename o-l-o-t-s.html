Our Lady of The Snakes

2016

The notorious antagonist from The Book of Hester Issue 1, Hester and the Search for Truth. It is she who tells Hester that there is no truth. Furthermore, she points out that there is no Evil and there is no Good, there is only the unrelenting mixing of them both. She tells Hester that in order to overcome his shame, he needs to embrace not only Good, but Evil too. With an air of Finality, Our Lady of the Snakes motions her arms across each other making mysterious signs. Immediately the two serpents at her shoulders slither down her arms, speedily making their way toward Hester. Although her words hold a few truths, she is without doubt deceptive. Throughout the Novel, the snakes are symbolic of Evil and Entropy- Our Lady of the Snakes is the mother of these things and she has no good intentions whatsoever.
